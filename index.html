    Hari Raya Aidilfitri
    Date: Mac 21–22, 2026
    Location: Our House or Hometown

    Aidilfitri, also known as Eid al-Fitr, is a celebration that marks the end of Ramadan. It is a joyful time when Muslims gather with family and friends, seek forgiveness, enjoy special meals, and wear traditional clothes like Baju Melayu and Baju Kurung to celebrate together.

    Activities include:

    • Aidilfitri prayers
    • Visiting family and relatives
    • Giving ‘duit raya’ (money packets)
    • Enjoying festive food


    Chineese New Year
    Date: April 15–17, 2025
    Location: Our House or Hometown

    Chinese New Year is a festival that celebrates the start of a new year in the Chinese calendar. Families come together, wear red clothes, give red packets, and enjoy special food and traditions.

    Activities include:

    • Family reunion dinner
    • Giving red packets (angpao)
    • Wearing red clothes
    • Lion and dragon dances


    Independence Day
    Date: August 31
    Location: Putrajaya

    Hari Kemerdekaan, or Independence Day, is a special day when Malaysians celebrate the country’s freedom from British rule on August 31. People show their love for Malaysia with flags, parades, and patriotic events.

    Activities include:

    • Raising the Malaysian flag
    • Watching the national parade
    • Decorating with flags and posters


    Christmas Day
    Date: December 25
    Location: Our House and church

    Christmas Day is a special holiday on December 25 that celebrates the birth of Jesus Christ. People enjoy time with family, give gifts, decorate trees, and share festive meals.

    Activities include:

    • Decorating the Christmas tree
    • Exchanging gifts
    • Attending church services


    Deepavali
    Date: November 8
    Location: Our House

    Deepavali, also called Diwali, is the Festival of Lights celebrated by Hindus. People wear traditional clothes like sarees and kurta, light oil lamps, and make and eat sweets such as laddoo, murukku, and halwa.

    Activities include:

    • Lighting oil lamps (diyas)
    • Cleaning and decorating the house
    • Praying at temples


    Hari Gawai
    Date: June 1
    Location: Sarawak

    Hari Gawai is a harvest festival celebrated by the Dayak people of Sarawak. It is a time to give thanks for the rice harvest, wear traditional clothes, enjoy traditional food and drinks like lemang and tuak, and celebrate with family and friends through dance, music, and cultural activities.

    Activities include:

    • Wearing traditional Iban or Bidayuh clothes
    • Performing traditional dances
    • Drinking and sharing rice wine
